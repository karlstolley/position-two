Position and Images

  • It’s All Illusion

    CSS positioning coupled with background images leads to compelling page designs. Just remember, though: it’s all an illusion, a matter of hiding some things, revealing others, and sliding it all into place.

  • One Image Per Element

    Until some of the specifications of CSS3 for border-image become more stable, we can only reliably design with one background image per element. Use them wisely—and pay attention to sibling and parent elements, and how they might aid the design of their siblings or children.

  • Break Out of the Box

    All elements appear as boxes; but your images need not. The key to breaking the boxy look that the box model encourages is to use images that violate the lines of boxes, applying extra padding where necessary.

  • Expect Expanding Content

    Although it is tempting to make complete images, with tops, bottoms, and sides, content expands and contracts. Seek out repeating images where this may occur, and make use of sibling and parent images to add top and bottom images.
